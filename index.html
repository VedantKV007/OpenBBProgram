Enigma Machine as AI Analogy

The Enigma machine was an electro-mechanical cipher device used during World War II to encrypt military communications. It worked using rotating rotors, electrical pathways, and configurable settings that transformed input letters into constantly changing outputs.

Its design relied on multiple layers of transformation including plugboard swaps, rotor mappings, and reflection. Each keystroke altered the internal state, making encryption dynamic and difficult to predict.

Despite its complexity, weaknesses such as predictable operator behavior and structural patterns allowed Allied cryptanalysts to break the system, laying foundations for modern computing and cryptography.
